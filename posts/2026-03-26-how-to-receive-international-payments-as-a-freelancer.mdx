## What You Need to Get Started
To start receiving international payments as a freelancer, you'll need to have a few things in place. First, you'll need to have a professional online presence, including a website and social media profiles, to attract and engage with potential clients. You'll also need to have a clear understanding of your services and pricing, as well as a contract or agreement that outlines the terms of your work. Here are some of the key things you'll need to get started:
* A **professional website** that showcases your skills and services, and allows clients to contact you and learn more about your work.
* A **payment platform** that allows you to receive international payments, such as PayPal, Payoneer, or TransferWise.
* A **contract or agreement** that outlines the terms of your work, including payment terms, deadlines, and scope of work.
* A **tax identification number** that allows you to receive payments and report your income to the tax authorities.
* A **bank account** that allows you to receive and manage your payments, and to transfer funds to your local account.

## Step-by-Step Guide
Here's a step-by-step guide to receiving international payments as a freelancer:
1. **Choose a payment platform**: Research and choose a payment platform that meets your needs, such as PayPal, Payoneer, or TransferWise. Consider factors such as fees, processing times, and security when making your decision.
2. **Create an account**: Sign up for an account with your chosen payment platform, and provide the required information, such as your name, address, and tax identification number.
3. **Verify your account**: Verify your account by providing identification documents, such as a passport or driver's license, and linking your bank account or debit card.
4. **Set up your payment terms**: Set up your payment terms, including your pricing, payment schedule, and payment method, and communicate these to your clients.
5. **Invoice your clients**: Invoice your clients for your work, using a professional invoice template that includes your payment terms and payment platform details.
6. **Receive and manage your payments**: Receive and manage your payments through your payment platform, and transfer funds to your local account as needed.
