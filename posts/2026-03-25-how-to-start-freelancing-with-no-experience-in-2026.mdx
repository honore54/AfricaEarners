## What You Need to Get Started
Before you can start freelancing, there are a few things you'll need to get started. These include a computer or laptop, internet access, and a few basic tools and software. Here are some of the key things you'll need to get started:
* A **reliable computer or laptop** with a good processor, memory, and storage. This will be your primary tool for completing freelance work, and you'll need to ensure it's capable of handling the demands of your projects.
* **Internet access** with a fast and stable connection. This will allow you to communicate with clients, access online platforms and tools, and complete work efficiently.
* **Basic software and tools**, such as a word processor, spreadsheet software, and design tools. The specific software and tools you'll need will depend on the type of freelance work you're doing, but having a basic set of tools will help you get started.
* A **professional online presence**, including a website or profile on freelance platforms. This will help you showcase your skills and services to potential clients, and make it easy for them to find and hire you.
* **Good communication and time management skills**, which are essential for succeeding as a freelancer. You'll need to be able to communicate effectively with clients, manage your time efficiently, and prioritize your work to meet deadlines.

## Step-by-Step Guide
Here's a step-by-step guide to help you **start freelancing no experience**:
1. **Identify your skills and strengths**, and determine what type of freelance work you want to do. This could be writing, designing, developing, or consulting, among other options. Take some time to reflect on your skills, experience, and interests, and think about how you can apply them to freelance work.
2. **Choose a freelance platform or website** to showcase your services and find clients. There are many platforms to choose from, including Upwork, Freelancer, and Fiverr. Research each platform, and choose the one that best fits your needs and goals.
3. **Create a professional profile**, including a portfolio and description of your services. This will help you stand out from other freelancers, and attract potential clients. Make sure your profile is complete, up-to-date, and showcases your skills and experience.
4. **Develop a personal brand**, including a website or blog to promote your services. This will help you establish yourself as an expert in your field, and attract clients who are looking for high-quality freelance work. Take the time to develop a strong brand, and make sure it's consistent across all your online platforms.
5. **Set your rates and payment terms**, and be prepared to negotiate with clients. This can be a challenging part of freelancing, but it's essential to get right. Research your competition, and set rates that reflect your skills and experience.
6. **Start applying for freelance jobs**, and be prepared to face rejection. Don't get discouraged if you don't land a job right away – keep applying, and eventually you'll find a client who's a good fit for your skills and services. Make sure you're applying for jobs that match your skills and experience, and tailor your application to each job.

## How Much Can You Realistically Earn
The amount you can earn as a freelancer depends on your skills, experience, and the type of work you're doing. **Start freelancing no experience**, and you can expect to earn lower rates, at least initially. However, as you gain experience and build your portfolio, you can increase your rates and earn more. Here are some realistic earnings ranges for freelancers:
* **Beginner freelancers**, with little or no experience, can expect to earn $15-$25 per hour, or $1,000-$2,000 per month.
* **Intermediate freelancers**, with some experience and a established portfolio, can expect to earn $25-$50 per hour, or $2,000-$5,000 per month.
* **Advanced freelancers**, with extensive experience and a strong reputation, can expect to earn $50-$100 per hour, or $5,000-$10,000 per month.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
Here are 4 common mistakes to avoid as a freelancer:
* **Not having a clear contract or agreement** with clients, which can lead to misunderstandings and disputes. Make sure you have a clear contract, and that both you and the client understand the terms and conditions.
* **Not setting clear boundaries** with clients, which can lead to scope creep and burnout. Make sure you set clear boundaries, and communicate them clearly to your clients.
* **Not prioritizing self-care**, which can lead to burnout and decreased productivity. Make sure you prioritize your physical and mental health, and take breaks when you need them.
* **Not continuously marketing and promoting** your services, which can lead to a lack of clients and income. Make sure you continuously market and promote your services, and stay visible to potential clients.
