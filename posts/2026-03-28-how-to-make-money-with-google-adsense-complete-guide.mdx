## What You Need to Get Started
To start making money with Google AdSense, you'll need to meet certain requirements and have the right tools and resources in place. Before you can apply for the program, you'll need to make sure your website or blog meets the eligibility criteria, which includes having a significant amount of original and high-quality content, as well as complying with Google's terms of service and program policies. Here are the key requirements to get started:
* A **fully functional website or blog** with a clear and easy-to-use navigation, as well as a sufficient amount of content to attract and engage visitors.
* **Original and high-quality content** that adds value to your audience and is free from copyrighted or infringing material.

## Step-by-Step Guide
Here's a step-by-step guide to getting started with Google AdSense:
1. **Create a Google account** if you don't already have one, and make sure you have a valid email address and password. This will be used to access your AdSense dashboard and manage your account.
2. **Apply for the Google AdSense program** by filling out the online application form, which will ask for information about your website, content, and audience. Make sure you meet the eligibility criteria before applying.
3. **Wait for approval**, which can take anywhere from a few days to several weeks, depending on the volume of applications and the complexity of your site. You'll receive an email notification once your account is approved.
4. **Set up your AdSense account** by logging in to your dashboard and completing your profile, including your payment information and tax details. This will ensure that you receive your earnings on time and in the correct amount.
5. **Create ad units** and add them to your website or blog, using the ad code provided by Google. You can choose from a variety of ad formats, including display ads, link units, and native ads.
6. **Optimize your ad performance** by experimenting with different ad placements, sizes, and formats, as well as monitoring your earnings and adjusting your strategy accordingly. This will help you maximize your revenue and improve your user experience.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
Here are 4 common mistakes to avoid when using Google AdSense:
* **Clicking on your own ads**, which is against Google's terms of service and can result in your account being suspended or terminated. Make sure you have a clear understanding of the program policies and avoid any behavior that could be seen as fraudulent or manipulative.
* **Using ad placements that are not compliant**, such as placing ads on pages with little or no content, or using ad formats that are not allowed, such as pop-ups or overlays. Make sure you review the program policies and guidelines carefully before adding ads to your site.
* **Ignoring your website's user experience**, which can lead to a decrease in ad revenue and a poor user experience, as visitors are more likely to click on ads that are relevant and non-intrusive. Make sure your site is easy to navigate, with minimal clutter and a clear focus on your content.
* **Not disclosing your affiliation with Google AdSense**, which is required by the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) and can result in fines or penalties if not complied with. Make sure you include a clear disclosure statement on your site, indicating that you are a participant in the Google AdSense program.
