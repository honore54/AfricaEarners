## What You Need to Get Started
To start using Wise for international money transfers, you'll need to set up an account and verify your identity. This process is relatively straightforward, but it's essential to have the right documents and information ready. Here's what you'll need to get started:
* A valid government-issued ID, such as a passport or national ID card
* Proof of address, such as a utility bill or bank statement
* A bank account or mobile money wallet to receive and send funds
* An email address and phone number to verify your account
* A computer or mobile device with internet access to set up and manage your account

## Step-by-Step Guide
Here's a step-by-step guide to using Wise for international money transfers:
1. Sign up for a Wise account on the platform's website or mobile app. This will require you to provide some basic information, such as your name, email address, and password. Once you've signed up, you'll receive a verification email to confirm your account.
2. Verify your identity by uploading a copy of your government-issued ID and proof of address. This is a standard security measure to prevent fraud and ensure that your account is secure.
3. Set up your bank account or mobile money wallet to receive and send funds. You'll need to provide your account details, including the account number and routing number, to link your account to Wise.
4. Create a transfer by selecting the currency you want to send and the amount you want to transfer. You'll also need to enter the recipient's details, including their name, email address, and account information.
5. Review the transfer details and fees before confirming the transaction. Wise will provide you with a breakdown of the fees and exchange rates, so you can see exactly how much you'll be charged.
6. Fund the transfer using your linked bank account or mobile money wallet. You can also use a credit or debit card to fund the transfer, but be aware that this may come with additional fees.
