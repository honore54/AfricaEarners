Twitter — now called X — is paying creators real money through its **Ad Revenue Sharing program**, and yes, it works for Africans including Rwandans. Your friend was right.

## What You Need to Know About Twitter Impressions

Twitter pays you a share of the ad revenue generated from ads shown in the reply threads of your posts. The key thing most people miss: **only impressions from verified (Premium) users count**. Regular free users watching your content don't generate income.

This means it's not just about posting viral content — you need to build an audience where a meaningful portion of your followers have Twitter Premium subscriptions.

## Step-by-Step Guide to Get Started

**Step 1: Subscribe to Twitter/X Premium**
This costs around $8/month and is mandatory. Think of it as your business license for monetization.

**Step 2: Grow to 500+ followers**
Focus on your niche. Posting about tech, money, and online work attracts other professionals who are more likely to have Premium accounts.

**Step 3: Hit 5 million impressions in 3 months**
This sounds huge but isn't impossible. Consistent daily posting, engaging with trending topics, and posting at peak hours (morning and evening) builds impressions fast.

**Step 4: Apply in Creator Studio**
Go to Twitter Creator Studio → Monetization → Ad Revenue Sharing → Apply.

**Step 5: Connect Stripe or link to Payoneer**
For Africans, Payoneer is your best option since Stripe isn't fully available in Rwanda yet.

## How Much Can You Earn?

The numbers are real but the range is wide:

- **Small accounts (5K followers):** $50–$200/month
- **Mid accounts (50K followers):** $300–$2,000/month  
- **Large accounts (500K+ followers):** $5,000–$20,000/month

Your friend's girlfriend earning $2,000 in two weeks suggests she has a large, engaged following of verified users. It's not typical for beginners but absolutely possible with time.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Focusing on total impressions, not verified impressions.** You might have 10 million impressions but only 500K from Premium users — that's what actually pays.

**Inconsistent posting.** The algorithm rewards daily activity. Going quiet for a week drops your reach significantly.

**Wrong content type.** Text threads and long-form posts with high engagement earn more than random short tweets.

**Not tracking your analytics.** Check Twitter Analytics weekly to see which posts generated the most Premium impressions.

## Tips for Africans Specifically

**Post about topics that attract global verified users.** Tech, programming, business, finance, and entrepreneurship attract more Premium subscribers than local-only content.

**Use English primarily.** Most Premium users are from the US, UK, and Europe. English content reaches this high-value audience.

**Post at US business hours too.** 9 AM–12 PM EST is peak Twitter time in the US — scheduling posts for this window increases Premium user views even from Rwanda.

**Build your profile properly.** Add a clear photo, compelling bio, and link to your website. Professional profiles get followed by professional (often Premium) users.

## Conclusion

Twitter monetization is legitimate and it works for Africans. The challenge is patience — you won't earn $2,000 in two weeks when you're starting with 3 followers. But with consistent posting over 6–12 months, building to 10K+ followers in the right niche, and creating content that attracts professional audiences, it becomes a real income stream.
