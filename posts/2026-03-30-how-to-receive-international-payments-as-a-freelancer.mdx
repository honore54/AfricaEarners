## What You Need to Get Started

You'll also need a way to invoice your clients and track your payments. Here are some of the key things you'll need to get started:

## Step-by-Step Guide
Here's a step-by-step guide to **receiving international payments as a freelancer**:
1. **Choose a payment platform**: Research and choose a payment platform that specializes in **receiving international payments**, such as PayPal or Payoneer. Consider factors like fees, payout times, and ease of use.
2. **Create a professional website**: Build a professional website that showcases your services and portfolio. This will help you to attract clients and establish your credibility as a freelancer.
3. **Set up your payment platform**: Sign up for your chosen payment platform and set up your account. This will typically involve providing some basic information, such as your name and address.
4. **Create an invoice template**: Create an invoice template that you can use to bill your clients. This should include your payment details, as well as the amount and description of the work.
5. **Send your invoice**: Send your invoice to your client and wait for payment. Make sure to follow up if you don't receive payment within a few days.
6. **Track your payments**: Use your payment platform to track your payments and stay on top of your finances. This will help you to identify any issues or discrepancies.

## How Much Can You Realistically Earn
The amount you can earn as a freelancer depends on a variety of factors, including your skills, experience, and the type of work you do. **Beginner freelancers** can expect to earn around $15-$25 per hour, while **intermediate freelancers** can earn $25-$50 per hour. **Advanced freelancers** can earn $50-$100 per hour or more.
