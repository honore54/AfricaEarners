## What Is Payoneer vs Wise and Why It Matters
Payoneer and Wise are two leading online payment platforms that enable freelancers to receive payments from clients worldwide. **Payoneer** is a digital payment platform that provides a range of services, including online money transfers, debit cards, and payment processing. Founded in 2005, Payoneer has become a popular choice among freelancers, online marketplaces, and businesses looking to send and receive international payments. On the other hand, **Wise** (formerly TransferWise) is a peer-to-peer payment platform that allows individuals and businesses to send and receive money across borders at a lower cost. Wise was founded in 2011 and has since become known for its transparent fees and competitive exchange rates.

The main difference between Payoneer and Wise lies in their business models and fee structures. Payoneer charges a fee for each transaction, which can range from 2% to 5% depending on the type of transaction and the recipient's location. Wise, on the other hand, charges a small fee for each transaction, which is typically lower than Payoneer's fees. However, Wise's fees can add up for large transactions, making Payoneer a more cost-effective option for big-ticket payments. Understanding the differences between Payoneer and Wise is crucial for freelancers, as it can help them choose the best platform for their business needs and avoid unnecessary fees.

In addition to their fee structures, Payoneer and Wise also differ in terms of their features and services. Payoneer offers a range of services, including online invoicing, payment tracking, and debit cards, which can be useful for freelancers who need to manage their finances on the go. Wise, on the other hand, focuses on providing fast and secure international money transfers, with a user-friendly interface and transparent fees. Ultimately, the choice between Payoneer and Wise will depend on your specific business needs and preferences.
