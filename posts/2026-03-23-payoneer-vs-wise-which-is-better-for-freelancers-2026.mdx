## What Is Payoneer vs Wise and Why It Matters
Payoneer and Wise are two popular online payment platforms that cater to freelancers, entrepreneurs, and small business owners. **Payoneer** is a digital payment platform that provides users with a virtual bank account, enabling them to receive payments from clients and customers worldwide. With Payoneer, you can receive payments in multiple currencies, convert them to your local currency, and withdraw the funds to your bank account or use them to make online purchases. On the other hand, **Wise** (formerly known as TransferWise) is a borderless payment platform that allows users to send and receive money across borders with minimal fees and exchange rates. Wise is known for its transparent and low-cost transactions, making it an attractive option for freelancers who work with international clients.

The main difference between Payoneer and Wise lies in their business models and target audiences. Payoneer is geared towards freelancers and small business owners who need to receive payments from clients, while Wise is focused on individuals and businesses that need to send and receive money across borders. Both platforms have their strengths and weaknesses, and understanding these differences is crucial for making an informed decision. For instance, Payoneer offers a more comprehensive suite of tools and services for freelancers, including invoicing and expense tracking, whereas Wise excels in its exchange rates and transfer fees.

The choice between Payoneer and Wise ultimately depends on your specific needs and requirements as a freelancer. If you primarily work with clients in your local market and need a robust invoicing and payment system, Payoneer might be the better choice. However, if you work with international clients and need to send and receive money across borders, Wise could be the more suitable option. By understanding the features and benefits of each platform, you can make an informed decision and choose the platform that best aligns with your business needs.

## What You Need to Get Started
To get started with either Payoneer or Wise, you'll need to meet certain requirements and provide some basic information. Before signing up, it's essential to understand the necessary steps and documents required to create an account. Here are the requirements you'll need to get started:
* **Valid government-issued ID**: You'll need to provide a valid government-issued ID, such as a passport or driver's license, to verify your identity.
* **Proof of address**: You'll need to provide a proof of address, such as a utility bill or bank statement, to confirm your address.
* **Business registration documents**: If you're a business owner, you'll need to provide business registration documents, such as articles of incorporation or a business license.
* **Bank account information**: You'll need to provide your bank account information to link your account to Payoneer or Wise.
* **Tax identification number**: You may need to provide your tax identification number, such as an EIN or SSN, to comply with tax regulations.

## Step-by-Step Guide
Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started with Payoneer and Wise:
1. **Sign up for an account**: Go to the Payoneer or Wise website and sign up for an account by providing your email address and password. You'll receive a verification email to confirm your account.
2. **Verify your identity**: Upload a valid government-issued ID and proof of address to verify your identity. This step is crucial to comply with anti-money laundering regulations.
3. **Link your bank account**: Provide your bank account information to link your account to Payoneer or Wise. This will enable you to receive payments and withdraw funds.
4. **Set up your payment settings**: Configure your payment settings, such as your payment methods and currency preferences. You can also set up invoicing and expense tracking with Payoneer.
5. **Create a borderless account**: With Wise, you can create a borderless account that allows you to hold and manage multiple currencies. This feature is ideal for freelancers who work with international clients.
6. **Test your account**: Once you've set up your account, test it by sending a small payment to yourself or a friend. This will ensure that your account is working correctly and that you can receive payments without any issues.
