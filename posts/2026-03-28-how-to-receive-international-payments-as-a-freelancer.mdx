## What You Need to Get Started
To get started with receiving international payments as a freelancer, you'll need a few essential tools and resources. These include a payment gateway, a bank account, and a way to invoice your clients. Here are some of the key requirements:
* A **payment gateway** such as PayPal, Stripe, or Payoneer, which allows you to receive payments from clients online.
* A **bank account** that can receive international payments, such as a borderless account or a multi-currency account.
* A **way to invoice your clients**, such as an invoicing software or a template, which allows you to send professional invoices and track your payments.
* A **contract or agreement** with your clients, which outlines the terms and conditions of your work, including payment terms and deadlines.
* A **tax ID number** or other identification, which is required for tax purposes and to comply with anti-money laundering regulations.

## Step-by-Step Guide
Here's a step-by-step guide on how to receive international payments as a freelancer:
1. **Choose a payment gateway**: Research and choose a payment gateway that suits your needs, such as PayPal, Stripe, or Payoneer. Consider factors such as fees, payment limits, and supported currencies.
2. **Create a payment account**: Sign up for a payment account with your chosen payment gateway, and follow the verification process to ensure that your account is secure and compliant with regulations.
3. **Set up your invoicing system**: Choose an invoicing software or template, and set up your invoicing system to send professional invoices to your clients. Make sure to include all the necessary details, such as payment terms and deadlines.
4. **Add your payment details to your invoice**: Include your payment details, such as your payment gateway account information, on your invoice, so that your clients can easily pay you online.
5. **Send your invoice to your client**: Send your invoice to your client, either manually or automatically, depending on your invoicing system.
6. **Track your payments**: Use your payment gateway and invoicing system to track your payments, and follow up with your clients if there are any issues or delays.
